\section{Study Design}

\gls{TILDA} is a prospective cohort study examining the social, economic, and health circumstances of community dwelling adults aged 50 years and older in Ireland. The present study extends this design by incorporating data from five waves of TILDA, as well as the external RAND harmonised dataset, which aligns TILDA with comparable UK and US cohorts.

\section{Cohort Definition}

The analysis included all participants who provided data in at least one of the \gls{TILDA} waves. This thesis retained the full eligible sample to maximise statistical power for machine learning. Participants were excluded only when they had no usable data after preprocessing (i.e., all features were missing or non informative).

No exclusions were applied based on age, medical conditions, cognitive status, or attendance at the health assessment. Filtering occurred primarily at the \emph{variable level}, where features with excessive missingness, no variation, or non informative structure were removed (see Chapter~\ref{chap:Implementation Details}).

\subsection{Inclusion/Exclusion criteria}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Participant Inclusion:} All respondents who contributed data in at least one of the TILDA waves were included, regardless of age.
    \item \textbf{Usable Data Requirement:} Participants were excluded only when every available feature was missing or non-informative after preprocessing.
    \item \textbf{Longitudinal Retention:} Individuals were retained even if they participated in only a subset of waves, enabling maximum dataset utilisation.
    \item \textbf{No Health or Cognitive Exclusions:} No participant was removed on the basis of medical diagnoses, cognitive scores, mobility limitations, or non-attendance at the health centre assessment.
\end{itemize}

The filtering process was therefore driven by data integrity rather than clinical eligibility. Figure~\ref{fig:variable_filter_criteria}.

\section{Data Collection}

Data were collected across five waves:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Wave 1:} 2009-2011
    \item \textbf{Wave 2:} 2012-2013
    \item \textbf{Wave 3:} 2014-2015
    \item \textbf{Wave 4:} 2016-2018
    \item \textbf{Wave 5:} 2018-2020
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Note:} The Harmonised dataset was obtained from the \gls{RAND} \gls{TILDA} repository and is designed to align \gls{TILDA} with comparable surveys such as \gls{RAND} gls{HRS}, \gls{ELSA}, and \gls{SHARE}. At present, the harmonised version is limited to Waves 1 and 2 and includes variables from the Demographics, Family, and Health sections. 
This dataset contains cleaned and processed variables with consistent naming conventions and model based imputations where applicable. It is included here for reference and comparability with other longitudinal aging studies, but it is \emph{not} the output of the preprocessing and cleaning pipeline applied to the full five wave dataset used in this thesis for machine learning analyses.

Each wave included three components:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{\gls{CAPI}:} Home based, computer assisted personal interviews
    \item \textbf{\gls{SCQ}:} A paper based self completion survey focused on mental health, lifestyle, and subjective well being
    \item \textbf{Health Assessment:} Clinical and biomarker measurements in centers or at home
\end{enumerate}
These criteria ensured that participants could provide reliable data for longitudinal analyses.

\begin{itemize}
    \item inspecting datatypes for every column,
    \item quantifying missingness, sentinel values, and unique entries,
    \item generating descriptive statistics (mean, median, min, max),
    \item and visuals (histograms, distributions) to identify anomalies.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Variable Filtering Criteria}

The manual variable filtering process involved applying a set of predefined thresholds to remove non-informative or structurally invalid variables prior to automated preprocessing. These criteria included thresholds for missingness, uniqueness, dominant category frequency, datatype quality, and administrative identifiers (see mapping table in Appendix~\ref{appendix_variable_filtering}).
